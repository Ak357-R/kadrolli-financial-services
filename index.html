SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a disciplined way to invest a fixed amount regularly — monthly or weekly — into mutual funds. It helps you build wealth over time by averaging the cost of investment and benefiting from the power of compounding, without worrying about market timing.

Absolutely. SIPs can begin with as little as ₹500 per month. The key is to start early and stay consistent. Even modest amounts invested regularly can grow significantly over a 10–15 year horizon thanks to the magic of compounding.

Mutual funds give you access to a diversified portfolio managed by professional fund managers — without needing deep market expertise yourself. They spread risk across many assets, are regulated by SEBI, and offer options for every risk appetite and financial goal.

At least once a year, or whenever your financial situation or goals change significantly — like a new job, marriage, child, or major expense. Regular reviews help ensure your investments stay aligned with your evolving life goals.

Yes — mutual funds, especially equity-oriented ones, are among the most effective long-term wealth creation tools available. A well-structured retirement SIP started in your 30s can accumulate a significant corpus by the time you retire, often outpacing traditional instruments.

ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) is a mutual fund category that qualifies for tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. You can invest up to ₹1.5 lakh per year and claim deductions, potentially saving up to ₹46,800 in taxes — while also growing your money in equity markets. It has the shortest lock-in period (3 years) among all 80C options.
